Rides the Wake carries herself like someone accustomed to moving through rough water — patient in stillness, overwhelming in motion.

She is not reckless in the way people imagine barbarians to be. There is a current of deliberate purpose beneath the fury, a sense that the rage, when it comes, has been earned. Those who travel with her describe a quiet confidence that borders on the serene, right up until it doesn't. The name fits: she goes where the force has been and holds the line in the turbulence that follows.

---

### Through Two Shards

In Shard A — the Wild Reaches — the answer to most problems was direct and physical. The ancient forest and its feral guardians required something capable of absorbing enormous punishment while delivering worse in return. Rides the Wake provided that. Path of the Berserker means Frenzy is available — bonus action attacks, sustained offensive pressure — at the cost of Exhaustion afterward. She does not use it casually. In Shard A, there were moments when she did, and the party learned to read those moments as a reliable signal that things had become genuinely dire.

Shard B was stranger and more disorienting — corruption doesn't offer the same clean physical opposition as a feral spirit or an overgrown predator. But Danger Sense (advantage on DEX saves against visible threats) and the resistance to physical damage that Rage provides don't stop working just because the threat is uncanny. She was the one who could wade through what Shard B offered while others fell back. Her constitution is not merely biological. It is philosophical.

She came out of the Corrupted Depths at 68 hit points and she will walk into the Sea of Governance at 68 hit points. Whatever happened in there did not leave a mark she is showing.

---

### In the Field

**Role:** Primary frontline striker. First choice when something needs to hit very hard, very fast. Rage provides STR bonus to damage and resistance to physical damage — she becomes something that fights better the worse the situation gets, which is a useful quality to have in a party.

**The trick:** She is not the last resort. She is the first response. The Berserker who is also Patient is rarer than it sounds. In practice, this means she reads the fight before entering it and enters it at a moment of her choosing rather than the enemy's.

---

### At Shard C

The Shard C — the Sea of Governance — has been reached at last, and if the effort of getting here shows on the others, it does not show on her. She was present for the arrival in the way she is present for most things: fully, without announcement.

Whatever waits in a sea named for governance and power, she has not dressed it up into something more than it is. A place is a place. You enter it. You find out what it asks of you. The Sea of Governance may be a place where power wears formal dress. She has never needed that kind of armor.

The party is resupplied, rested, and ready. Rides the Wake is simply ready.
