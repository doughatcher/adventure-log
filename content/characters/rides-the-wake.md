Rides the Wake carries herself like someone accustomed to moving through rough water — patient in stillness, overwhelming in motion.

She is not reckless in the way people imagine barbarians to be. There is a current of deliberate purpose beneath the fury, a sense that the rage, when it comes, has been earned. Those who travel with her describe a quiet confidence that borders on the serene, right up until it doesn't. The name fits: she goes where the force has been and holds the line in the turbulence that follows.

In Shard A — the Wild Reaches — the answer to most problems was direct and physical. The ancient forest and its feral guardians required something capable of absorbing enormous punishment while delivering worse in return. Rides the Wake provided that. Path of the Berserker means Frenzy is available — bonus action attacks, sustained offensive pressure — at the cost of Exhaustion afterward. She does not use it casually. In Shard B, where corruption ran deeper and the threats grew stranger, that same discipline kept the cost from coming due at the wrong moment.

The party has now crossed into Shard C — the Sea of Governance. Whatever waits there, it is something different from what came before. Wilder threats have given way to something more structured, more political, more difficult to answer with a blade. Rides the Wake has rested. She is ready. How readiness translates in a Shard named for governance rather than nature is a question she has not yet been asked to answer.
