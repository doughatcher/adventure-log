Rides the Wake carries herself like someone accustomed to moving through rough water — patient in stillness, overwhelming in motion.

She is not reckless in the way people imagine barbarians to be. There is a current of deliberate purpose beneath the fury, a sense that the rage, when it comes, has been earned. Those who travel with her describe a quiet confidence that borders on the serene, right up until it doesn't. The name fits: she goes where the force has been and holds the line in the turbulence that follows.

In Shard A — the Wild Reaches — the answer to most problems was direct and physical. The ancient forest and its feral guardians required something capable of absorbing enormous punishment while delivering worse in return. Rides the Wake provided that. Path of the Berserker means Frenzy is available — bonus action attacks, sustained offensive pressure — at the cost of Exhaustion afterward. She does not use it casually. In Shard A, there were moments that demanded it. In Shard B, she learned when not to.

Now the party stands at the edge of Shard C — the Sea of Governance. The threats here will be different: structured, political, armored in legitimacy rather than fang and root. Whether the Wake that crashes through bureaucracy looks the same as the one that crashed through feral wilderness remains to be seen. She is resting. She is ready. Those are not contradictory.
