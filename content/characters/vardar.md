Vardar gives the impression of someone who has seen enough of the world to be skeptical of it, but not so much as to have stopped caring. A Goliath bard of the College of Spirits — a strange combination that somehow makes complete sense once you encounter it. The spirits speak to those who are large enough to hold them, and Vardar has always been that.

He arrived at the Shard C alongside the others, and there was something in the way he processed the place — talking around it, noting its name with a kind of wary appreciation — that suggested a mind that understands power by narrating it. The Sea of Governance is a sea that has a story about itself, and Vardar is the sort of person who will want to know whether that story is true. Bards of the College of Spirits have learned that official histories and actual ones rarely overlap.

The name carries weather in it, or the memory of weather — a history with forces that do not ask permission. In a place called the Sea of Governance, that history will be tested. Whether the storm in him is literal, metaphorical, or something older than either, it remains a force that has learned to perform its nature. The Sea of Governance will learn what that means soon enough.
