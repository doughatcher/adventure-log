Granit is a name that suits someone who does not move unless they mean to.

A dwarf cleric of the Life Domain — healer, anchor, the kind of steadiness a party leans on before they realize they're leaning. There is an economy to how Granit operates: no wasted gestures, no unnecessary words, no faith expressed through volume or theater. The sacred power moving through them is not flashy, but it is deep, and it shows up when it is needed.

---

### Through Two Shards

Shard A — the Wild Reaches — established the rhythm. When the forests turned hostile and the feral spirits pressed in with a ferocity that had no bottom to it, it was Granit who kept the party from attrition. Not through heroics. Through the quiet, relentless work of making sure that no one bled out in the wrong moment. The Life Domain amplifies healing — Cure Wounds lands harder, Healing Word crosses greater distance — and in Shard A, that difference was the margin between a party that survived and one that didn't.

Shard B was harder in a different way. Corruption of essence doesn't announce itself with fire or teeth. It seeps. It changes the quality of sound and light, makes familiar spells feel uncertain, puts a strange weight on divine connection as though something in the local metaphysics disapproves. Granit's Channel Divinity — Preserve Life — is designed for emergencies, mass stabilization when the worst has already happened. In the Corrupted Depths, it was needed. That says enough about what they faced.

Coming out the other side of Shard B, Granit arrived at Shard C the same as they went in: whole, unhurried, and ready. That is either discipline or temperament. Probably both.

---

### In the Field

**Role:** Primary healer and divine anchor. Heavy armor means Granit stands in the thick of it — AC 18 at the front, keeping the line while the others do what they do. Blessed Healer means healing others also heals Granit; the cleric who heals the party is also the cleric who stays standing.

**The trick:** Granit does not announce what they're about to do. The spell is already cast before anyone has noticed the situation was critical. That's not luck. That is someone who has been paying attention to the shape of a fight for long enough to read where the damage will go.

---

### At Shard C

They arrived at the Sea of Governance alongside the others, and that silence of theirs said as much as any speech could. A sea named for power and law and institutional authority. The kind of opposition you can't simply outlast or outfight. Granit has always understood that not every threat has a throat.

The long rest is done. Spell slots restored. The party is as prepared as it is going to be. That is the kind of moment Granit was built for — not the arrival, not the glory, but the quiet work of making sure everyone is still standing when the accounting finally comes.
