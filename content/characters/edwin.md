Edwin arrived at the Shard C with the group, and there is something about him that suggests arrival is a thing he has done many times — in many senses of the word. He is the kind of person whose history you piece together from small details: a comment here, a hesitation there, the particular way he accounts for things. The Sea of Governance is a place where histories have weight, and Edwin's, whatever it is, will matter here.

He was present for the small rituals of preparation that closed out this leg of the journey — the settling of debts, the equipping of gear, the long rest that follows a hard road. There is something telling in how a person handles that kind of administrative moment, the unglamorous work of getting ready. Edwin handled it like someone who knows the value of being properly equipped before you find out you needed to be.

Whatever brought him to this party, whatever he carries that hasn't been named yet, he is here now. The Sea of Governance has a way of surfacing things. Edwin seems like a man who has considered that possibility and made his calculations accordingly.
