Lyvriele Enafrya moves through the world with the particular bearing of someone who has learned that elegance and survival are not mutually exclusive.

An Eladrin fighter trained in the Samurai tradition — a discipline as much about presence as technique, about the unsheathed quality of attention a warrior brings to everything, not just combat. The Samurai does not simply react to a fight. They have already considered it. Lyvriele does not draw her blade slowly because she is hesitant. She draws it slowly because the time spent not drawing it is when she has already decided.

---

### Through Two Shards

Shard A — the Wild Reaches — tested precision in an environment that did not reward it. When everything is overgrown and feral and the threat comes from every direction at once, a fighter who relies on placement and timing has to work differently than one who just swings. The Eladrin Fey Step — short-range teleportation as a bonus action — is an ability that sounds elegant in a tavern and is invaluable in a collapsing forest canopy full of hostile spirits. She used it constantly. She is still using it.

Shard B was an education in a different kind of attention. Corruption that operates on the level of essence — distorting the quality of perception, making familiar distances feel wrong — is exactly the kind of threat that a Samurai's discipline is designed to withstand. Fighting Spirit, the Samurai's signature ability, grants advantage on attacks and temporary hit points as a bonus action. In the Corrupted Depths, that meant maintaining offensive capability when the environmental conditions were actively fighting her concentration. She held her focus. The results showed.

She enters Shard C having equipped herself thoroughly at the last resupply: two new +1 weapons, a Cloak of Protection for that extra margin at AC. Lyvriele does not leave preparation to chance.

---

### In the Field

**Role:** Precision striker and action economy powerhouse. Action Surge adds a full additional action when the moment demands it. Extra Attack means two hits baseline, more with Action Surge. Fighting Spirit for advantage when she needs it. Fey Step for repositioning that ignores the terrain between her and where she needs to be.

**The trick:** She is the one who closes distance when everyone else is being cautious about it, and she is the one you notice closing it. Not because she makes noise. Because there is a particular quality of deliberateness to her movement that draws the eye.

---

### At Shard C

She watched the arrival at Shard C with the particular stillness of someone committing a place to memory before it changes. The Sea of Governance is named for a kind of order — structured, institutional, the sort of power that does not announce itself with thunder but with procedure.

For someone of Eladrin sensibility, that kind of power is neither foreign nor comfortable. It is simply another thing to read correctly. She has had two Shards to practice that. Whatever the Sea of Governance intends to ask of them, Lyvriele will have already considered the question before it is posed.
