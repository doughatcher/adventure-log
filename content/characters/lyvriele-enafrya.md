Lyvriele Enafrya moves through the world with the particular bearing of someone who has learned that elegance and survival are not mutually exclusive.

An Eladrin fighter trained in the Samurai tradition — a discipline as much about presence as technique, about the unsheathed quality of attention a warrior brings to everything, not just combat. The Samurai does not simply react to a fight. They have already considered it. Lyvriele does not draw her blade slowly because she is hesitant. She draws it slowly because the time spent not drawing it is when she has already decided.

Shard A — the Wild Reaches — tested precision in an environment that did not reward it. When everything is overgrown and feral and the threat comes from every direction at once, a fighter who relies on placement and timing has to work differently than one who just swings. The Eladrin Fey Step — short-range teleportation as a bonus action — is an ability that sounds elegant in a tavern and proves essential in a forest that is actively trying to kill you. She learned to use the unpredictability of the terrain rather than resist it. Shard B refined that further.

The Sea of Governance is a different kind of battlefield. Courts and hierarchies have their own geometries, and a Samurai understands something about the performance of controlled power — the message sent by stillness, by the weapon not drawn. Lyvriele enters Shard C rested, newly equipped, and with the quiet attention she brings to everything. She has already begun considering it.
