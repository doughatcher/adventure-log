Lyvriele Enafrya moves through the world with the particular bearing of someone who has learned that elegance and survival are not mutually exclusive.

An Eladrin fighter trained in the Samurai tradition — a discipline as much about presence as technique, about the unsheathed quality of attention a warrior brings to everything, not just combat. The Samurai does not simply react to a fight. They have already considered it. Lyvriele does not draw her blade slowly because she is hesitant. She draws it slowly because the time spent not drawing it is when she has already decided.

Shards A and B asked her to be precise in imprecise conditions — overgrown, feral, corrupted spaces where footing was uncertain and the threats did not respect spacing or timing. She adapted. Eladrin Fey Step is an ability that sounds elegant in a tavern and proves essential in a forest that is trying to kill you, or a Shard that has stopped behaving like itself. Lyvriele arrived at Shard C fully rested and, by all accounts, better armored than before — AC adjusted, equipment attuned, the kind of marginal improvements that add up to surviving the thing that would otherwise have ended it.

The Sea of Governance suggests a Shard shaped by structure, institution, the calcified weight of how power organizes itself. That is a different kind of field than the ones she has moved through. Lyvriele's tradition prizes composure above all things. If anything, she may be more at home here than she has been yet.
