Vargr Stormcaller gives the impression of someone who has seen enough of the world to be skeptical of it, but not so much as to have stopped caring.

A Goliath bard of the College of Spirits — a combination that sounds strange until you encounter it, and then seems inevitable. The spirits choose those who are large enough to hold them, and Vargr has always been that — physically, yes, but also in the way that matters more: in the breadth of what he is willing to consider, to witness, to carry. His Bardic Inspiration is the connective tissue of this party. When someone needs the extra measure of courage or skill that tips a bad situation into a survivable one, it comes from Vargr.

---

### Through Two Shards

Shard A — the Wild Reaches — was a revelation. Bards speak to the living and the recently dead, but a place with nature this ancient and this violent has memories of its own, and Vargr was the one sensitive to them. Tales from Beyond — channeling spirits through Bardic Inspiration to produce effects from the Great Tales table — works differently in a Shard than in an ordinary location. The spirits here had opinions. He listened. The party benefited from what he heard.

Shard B was where the College of Spirits earns its name. Corrupted ground changes the dead. They speak with more urgency, or less coherence, or with an agenda that the corruption has warped beyond recognition. Navigating that — pulling useful signal out of compromised sources — required the kind of interpretive skill that is not in any spell description. Vargr brought it anyway. There is a reason the party looks to him when they need to understand what a place has been, not just what it is.

He came out of the Corrupted Depths with new equipment — Ring of Swimming for the maritime arc ahead, a Cloak of Protection for that margin of survivability, a Luckstone for the moments when the dice simply cannot fall correctly. Vargr understands probability.

---

### In the Field

**Role:** Support, control, and knowledge. Jack of All Trades means he has at least some competence with every skill in the game; in practice, this means no gap is uncovered. Countercharm for charm and fear effects. College of Spirits Tales from Beyond for wild-card effects that can turn a fight. Spiritual Focus for spellcasting bonuses.

**The trick:** He is the one who names what the situation is, which is different from describing it. When something has gone wrong in a way no one can quite articulate, Vargr says it. That naming is a kind of power. Naming a thing correctly means you can respond to it correctly.

---

### At Shard C

At the threshold of Shard C, with the Sea of Governance spreading out before the party, Vargr was the one drawing connections aloud — tracing the shape of what this place might mean in the larger pattern of things.

The first Shard touched nature, raw and violent. The second corrupted something deeper. This one reaches into civilization itself, into the structures by which people agree to be governed at all. He named it in the way bards do: not because no one else had thought it, but because someone had to say it, and because the thing named becomes the thing you can act on.

The name "Stormcaller" suggests a history with forces larger than a person. He works with them rather than against them. The Sea of Governance is exactly that kind of force. Vargr will not be surprised by it. That may be the most dangerous thing about him.
