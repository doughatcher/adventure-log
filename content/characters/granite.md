Granite is a name that suits someone who does not move unless they mean to. A dwarf cleric of the Life Domain — healer, anchor, the kind of steadiness a party leans on before they know they're leaning. There is an economy to how Granite operates: no wasted gestures, no unnecessary words. The sacred power moving through them is not flashy, but it is deep, and it shows up when it is needed.

They arrived at the Shard C, Sea of Governance, alongside the others — and there was something in that silence that said as much as any speech could. Among the chatter of arrival, the accounting of coin and gear, the small negotiations of a party settling in, Granite was the still point. Whatever lies ahead in a place where governance takes on weight and form, Granite has likely already made their peace with it. The party has not. That gap may prove useful.

A comment drifted through the group about gods permitting whatever one wishes — and Granite did not take the bait, or perhaps simply did not hear it as bait. The Life Domain does not concern itself with permission so much as with what endures. In a sea named for governance, that distinction may come to matter.
