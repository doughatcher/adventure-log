title: The Adventure Begins

The vessel cut through darkening waters as the day surrendered its last light, and what had begun as familiar ocean swells gave way to something older and stranger beneath the hull. The party stood at the rail in near silence — Rides the Wake with his broad hands gripping weathered wood, Granite squinting into the distance with the patient wariness of a man who has learned to distrust calm water, Livrielle still as carved ivory, Vardar and Edwin at their respective stations — each of them feeling the change before they could name it. The sea here did not simply move. It breathed, or something very like breathing, slow and vast and indifferent to the small wooden thing drifting across its face.

The Shard C Sea of Governance announced itself not with spectacle but with pressure — a heaviness in the chest, a faint ringing behind the ears, the sense that the currents running beneath the keel were braided through with intention older than any kingdom that had ever claimed sovereignty over these waters. Ancient energies coiled in the deep like sleeping serpents, and the light that filtered through the waves from below was not quite the right color. Whatever governed here had been governing for a very long time.

Rides the Wake rolled his shoulders as though shaking off a chill, though the air was not cold. Granite murmured something low and private, perhaps a ward, perhaps a prayer, perhaps merely the habit of a cleric who has learned to acknowledge powers he cannot yet measure. The others steadied themselves against the subtle wrongness of the place and said little. There was an understanding among them, wordless and immediate, that they had crossed a threshold. The journey that brought them here was behind them now. Whatever came next would be different in kind, not merely in degree.

The ship held its course into waters that hummed with concealed significance, and the night gathered around the party like a held breath. Somewhere below, in the lightless architecture of the deep, something ancient registered their arrival. Whether it cared remained, for the moment, an open question — but morning would bring decisions: supplies to secure, charts to study, steel to sharpen, and bodies to rest before whatever the Sea of Governance intended to reveal of itself. They had arrived. The hard part, as it always was, lay just ahead.
