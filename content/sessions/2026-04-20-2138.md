## Previously

Two Shards down. One to go.

**Shard A — The Wild Reaches** was the beginning. The party entered a zone of primal nature that did not care whether they were heroes. Ancient forests with feral spirits and ecological violence older than cities, a place where the rules of civilization simply did not apply. They carved through it anyway. They came out changed — not scarred, not broken, but *settled*. The kind of settled that comes from surviving something genuinely uncertain together. The working dynamic was established here: Granit as anchor, Rides the Wake as the point of the spear, Lyvriele as the precision behind the force, Vargr as the one who understood what they were moving through.

**Shard B — The Corrupted Depths** tested something different. Corruption at the level of essence doesn't announce itself with teeth or fire. It distorts. The quality of light changes. Familiar spells feel different in the casting. The dead speak with agendas the corruption has warped beyond recognition. Vargr was essential here — the College of Spirits navigating compromised sources, pulling signal out of noise, interpreting what the corrupted dead were actually trying to say beneath what they said. The party held. They cleared it. They crossed into open sea on the other side with the weight of two Shards behind them and the knowledge that the third would be harder in a different way again.

Between the Shards, the party reached **Level 7** — a milestone that redraws the math of what they can do and survive. Granit's healing output increases again. Rides the Wake hits harder, takes more. Lyvriele gains another layer of the Samurai's focused capability. Vargr's College of Spirits deepens. They enter the Sea of Governance at the peak of what they currently are.

## The Crossing

The waters changed before the ship did.

Something in the air grew heavier as the vessel pressed on — a pressure behind the eyes, a stillness that had no business sitting atop open sea. Then the horizon offered up its answer: the Shard C Sea of Governance, vast and ancient, its surface catching light in ways that seemed almost deliberate, as though the ocean itself were aware of being watched in return.

Rides the Wake stood at the prow as they crossed into those strange waters, the human barbarian's broad frame unmoving against the salt wind. Behind her, Granit muttered something low in the old Dwarven tongue — a prayer, perhaps, or a warning dressed as one. The Life Domain cleric had seen enough of the world to know that power this old rarely asked permission before making itself felt. The energies here were not hostile, not yet, but they were not indifferent either. They pressed against the hull and curled through the rigging like fingers testing the measure of what had arrived.

Lyvriele Enafrya kept her hand near her blade out of habit more than fear, her Eladrin eyes tracking the currents below the surface where shapes of light moved with unsettling purpose. Beside her, Vargr Stormcaller had gone uncharacteristically quiet, the Goliath bard listening — truly listening — as though the sea were whispering something just below the pitch a human ear could catch. Whatever it was, he did not yet share it.

The crossing was uneventful in the way that a held breath is uneventful. Nothing attacked. The sea did not rise against them. They were received, in whatever sense a geographic zone of concentrated institutional power receives anything, without violence.

They made anchor before the full dark came down.

## At Port

The resupply was methodical. The party knows what it is doing now — two Shards of operational experience means the logistics of arriving somewhere new and making ready for whatever follows have become practiced, almost ceremonial.

Gear was inspected and upgraded where resources allowed. Equipment was purchased. The ship was assessed for what it would need in these new waters. The DM confirmed that the **Sea of Governance** brings with it expanded access — the Shard's maritime networks mean a wider variety of ship weapon systems are available, even if a larger hull is not yet within reach. Cannons. Ballistas. Swivel guns for close defense.

And the party was informed that a **Wand of Wonder** awaits them at the next Traveler's Beacon — a gift from the DM to mark the level-up and the arrival at Shard C. The Wand of Wonder is exactly what it sounds like: one hundred possible effects, rolled randomly each use, ranging from the merely strange to the truly chaotic. The party received this news with varying degrees of enthusiasm.

**Tier 2 magic items** are now available for purchase at the next Beacon. The party has access to things that were out of reach before. They spent accordingly. Gold changed hands. Equipment improved.

The long rest was completed on arrival. Spell slots restored, Rage uses recovered, Bardic Inspiration ready. Whatever the Sea of Governance has waiting for them, they meet it whole.

## Looking Ahead

The crew rested. The weapons are sharpened. The charts are being studied.

Whatever law or power has named these waters still waits somewhere beneath, patient as tide, certain as depth. The party has survived nature's violence and corruption's rot. What they face now is something that wears a civilized face. The question of Shard C is not whether they can fight their way through it. The question is whether they can navigate it — and what kind of people they will be when the navigation is done.

*Tomorrow, the Sea of Governance begins in earnest.*
