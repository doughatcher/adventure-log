title: The Adventure Begins

The sea changed before the crew fully understood what they were sailing into. The waters darkened by degrees — not with the familiar shadow of storm clouds or the murk of shallows, but with something older, something that pressed against the hull as though the ocean itself had grown aware of their passage. Rides the Wake stood at the prow and felt it in the soles of his feet: a low, persistent vibration, like the thrum of a bowstring long after the arrow has flown. The others gathered at the rail in ones and twos, drawn by the same wordless instinct that pulls wolves from the tree line when lightning strikes close.

Vardar and Edwin spoke in low tones near the stern while Livrielle watched the water with the particular stillness of someone committing a thing to memory. Granite said nothing at all, which was its own kind of language. The currents here did not flow the way currents should — they coiled and shifted with a deliberateness that had no natural explanation, parting around the vessel as if making room, or perhaps as if sizing it up. More than one of them had heard rumors of the Shard C Sea of Governance, the kind of rumors passed around in dockside taverns by sailors who laughed too quickly when they told them.

The laughing made more sense now that they were here. It was a way of not saying what you actually saw.

The vessel held its course as the last of the ordinary world fell behind them. Whatever governance this sea claimed dominion over, it predated any kingdom any of them had studied, any throne any of them had served or defied. The energies moving through the deep water were not hostile, precisely — but they were not indifferent either. They had weight, and intention, and patience on a scale that made mortal urgency feel very small. The party made their preparations quietly: tallying what they had, taking stock of what they lacked, tending to blades and bodies and the careful business of being ready.

Night settled over the water like a held breath. Whatever waited ahead in the Sea of Governance, it would still be waiting come morning.
